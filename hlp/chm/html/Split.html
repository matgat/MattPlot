Split directive

The split directive allows to partition the original data columns, indicating with a seqeunce of numbers the proportion of slice sizes. For example the file:
 Split example
#split 1,4
   00
   01
   02
   03
   04
   05
   06
   07
   08
   09
Is equivalent to:
 Splitted
   00  02
   01  03
       04
       05
       06
       07
       08
       09
This directive is extended also in case of more columns (though I can't figure in which situation this can be useful). The following file:
 More columns
#split 2,1
   00  10
   01  11
   02  12
   03  13
   04  14
   05  15
Is equivalent to:
 Splitted
   00  04  10  14
   01  05  11  15
   02      12
   03      13
An automatic split is performed when an header declaring more columns as indexed ordinates is followed by a single column of data; in this case the single column will be automatically splitted according to specified indexes scale; for example the file:
 AutoSplit
#header <a([0.5])> <b([1])> <c([2])>
   00
   01
   02
   03
   04
   05
   06
Is equivalent to:
 AutoSplitted
#header <a([0.5])> <b([1])> <c([2])>
   00  04  06
   01  05
   02
   03
Or also, in the equivalent multiple session syntax:
 AutoSplitted
#header <a([0.5])>
   00
   01
   02
   03
#header <b([1])>
   04
   05
#header <c([2])>
   06
