Resample directive

The resample directive applies to all retrieved data columns proportionally to their size. It enables a postprocessing activity to reorder and undersample data according to a sequence of indexes provided as argument. A sequence is composed by a chain of intervals, each interval is an arithmetic progression of indexes, characterized by a starting index, a step (1 if not specified) and an ending index. The progression contains always first index but not the ending index; this is denoted by the enclosing brackets that must strictly be [). Indexes start from zero and can be given as hard numbers or sums:
  • [0:100) count from 0 to 99 (0,1,2,...99)
  • [6:2:13) count from 6 to 12 with step 2 (6,8,10,12)
  • [4-1:2+1:10+3) count from 3 to 12 with step 3 (3,6,9,12)
Particular importance has the maximum index, representing the size of data to which indexes are referred. Resample indexes are automatically rescaled (except step) when applied to columns having a different size, so choose wisely the resample argument size to avoid rounding errors. Note that in the previous example no rounding errors on rescaled indexes are occurred: in fact avoid these errors you must refer indexes of resample argument to the greatest common divisor of column sizes.
The resample argument notation supports also a step value; the following example tells MattPlot to skip odd rows:
 Undersample example
#resample [0:2:10)
   00  10  20  30
   01  11  21  31
   02  12  22  32
   03  13  23  33
   04  14  24  34
   05  15  25  35
   06  16  26  36
   07  17  27  37
   08  18  28  38
   09  19  29  39
Is equivalent to:
 Undersampled
   00  10  20  30
   02  12  22  32
   04  14  24  34
   06  16  26  36
   08  18  28  38
The resample feature does not (yet) support fractional steps (this would involve an interpolation function), so at present only undersampling is allowed.
